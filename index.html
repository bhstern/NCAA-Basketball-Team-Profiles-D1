Background

I grew up loving college basketball and have spent the last several years watching as many games as I can — not just as a fan, but as someone trying to understand the game at a deeper level. Somewhere along the way, that obsession met my professional background in strategy and analytics, and this project was born.

By day I work in Corporate Strategy at Harris Blitzer Sports & Entertainment, the organization behind the Philadelphia 76ers and New Jersey Devils, where I have spent the last three years working on strategic initiatives across the sports and entertainment portfolio. Before that, I was a strategy consultant at Deloitte. The through line across all of it has been the same: take complex data, find the signal, and make it useful for decision-makers.

I am now actively pursuing opportunities in college basketball operations — Graduate Assistant roles, and positions in recruiting, scouting, NIL, and roster construction at D1 programs. This tool is both a reflection of that work and a demonstration of how I think about the game.

About This Project

This dashboard is built on 11 years of team-level D1 college basketball data (2016–2026), combining Bart Torvik efficiency metrics with shot quality and profile data scraped, engineered, and assembled from scratch using Python. Every team, every season has roughly 50 variables with D1-wide percentiles, conference ranks, and power vs. mid-major context.

The goal was to build something that answers real basketball questions: How does this team score? Where are they vulnerable? Are they getting better or worse? How do they compare to the top teams in their conference? Clustering work is ongoing and will be integrated as a second phase.
