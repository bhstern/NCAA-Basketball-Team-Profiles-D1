Data Sources & Citation

Raw team statistics, adjusted efficiencies, roster metrics, and shooting splits were sourced from Bart Torvik's T-Rank database (barttorvik.com). All feature engineering — including shot profile construction, per-possession estimates, expected PPP (xPPP), shotmaking residuals, and descriptive layers (z-scores, percentiles, ranks) — was developed independently as part of this analytics pipeline.

Torvik, Bart. T-Rank College Basketball Statistics. barttorvik.com.

What Comes from Torvik

Adjusted efficiencies (adj_oe, adj_de, adj_em), tempo (adj._t, raw_t), Four Factors, roster metrics (height, experience, talent, SOS), and raw shooting splits are sourced directly from T-Rank.

What Was Engineered Independently

Shot profile construction (parsing made-attempt strings, computing FGA totals, shot distribution rates, and FG% by location), the full-season PPP system (estimated FTA/FTM, total points, offensive and defensive PPP), expected PPP (xPPP) based on shot distribution multiplied by league-average shot values by year, shotmaking residuals, residual differentials, and all descriptive layers (z-scores, percentiles, D1 ranks, conference ranks, power/mid-major ranks) were built independently.

Dataset Scope

11 seasons of D1 college basketball (2016–2026). Approximately 3,900 team-season records. 398 columns per record including raw stats, engineered features, and descriptive layers. Updated through the 2025–26 full season.
